How often should you change your oil?

The previous rule of thumb was you must conduct oil changes after a 3,000-mile run mark or three months in between oil changes. However, this idea is generally outdated. While it is salient to have oil changes to keep your vehicle running smoothly and efficiently, many vehicles do well after 3,000 miles.

But, it is not always the case. Some vehicles need oil changes more frequently than others. For instance, driving your vehicle at low speeds and frequently starting up your engine may warrant a need for an oil change as often as every 1,000 miles. This distinction is because vehicles that consistently run only 10 miles or below can collect condensation in the system, which breaks down the oil faster.

Conversely, several vehicles can drive up to 10,000 miles safely and efficiently. For instance, when you run synthetic oil and traverse longer distances on a relatively new vehicle, it is possible to drive up to 10,000 miles before changing your oil. However, you must use caution when deciding when to change your oil.
